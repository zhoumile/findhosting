Why Bluehost?

One thing is for a fact, numbers can never lie. The fact that over 850,000 people are using Bluehost for their blogs goes along way to show you just how credible the web hosting company is. After reading the pros and cons of Bluehost shared hosting, it is without a doubt the best web hosting provider. A number of arguments have been brought forward that attempt to explain why Bluehost deserves the number one spot. Here is why:

  • Any plan subscribed for with Bluehost comes with a number of benefits including a free domain name for life.
  • For US based customers can enjoy a 24/7 customer support. The team is dedicated towards offering the best customer support service. To achieve this they answer calls promptly with a 3 minute wait time and attend to of their customers needs.
  • With Bluehost, customers are given the privilege of hosting multiple domains and websites using their accounts. One user can host up to about 100 WordPress sites with their account.
  • As the user you also benefit from unlimited space and transfer bandwidth.
  • Price wise, the rates offered at Bluehost are one of the most competitive rates available in the market and you can avail coupons from Bluehost to save money.
  • The web hosting provider provides you with an automated backup system for your files and database.
  • With Bluehost, you get assurance that any WordPress update will be available to you within the first 24hrs of release. This way you get to stay abreast of any technological changes. The updates are very useful as it helps fix security issues and adds new features to your blog page.

What to Avoid for Your Web Hosting Needs

When trying to find an ideal web hosting company, there are a few things you need to avoid if in case you ever think of surviving in the industry. One of the most important things to avoid is using a freely hosted blog.

These are blogs that most people tend to turn to whenever they want to give start blogging. Some of the most common free hosted blogs include: Blogger, WordPress.com, Tumblr, Live Journal among others.

In as much as these sites are good for beginners who are yet to make it as professional bloggers, they are not good enough in as far as making money is concerned. These are some of the reasons as to why they are not good enough.

  • Limited control – you do not get the privilege of tweaking the codes that eventually make up for the framework of the site. It allows you add some features to your site and updates.
  • Not good Google ranking – a well documented fact is that Google rarely gives much weight to free blogs when giving their search results. The main reason given for this is that Google does not rate such sites as being authoritative. Bloggers who use their own domain names make their intentions clear to Google.

Whichever way you look at it Bluehost hosting offers cost effective web hosting services which should be comfortable for both business and personal blogging.
