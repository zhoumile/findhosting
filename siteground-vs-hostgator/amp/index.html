SiteGround Vs HostGator – Hosting Services Compared!

If you ask a professional who uses web hosting, he/she will definitely recognize SiteGround and HostGator! Such are the reputations created by both web hosts, in the course of time. Although the SiteGround vs HostGator confusion is in existence now, both companies had started the journey at different times. But, now, they are actually competitors to each other. Now, however, to help the newbie blogger / professional in you, we would do a comparison between SiteGround and HostGator, based on different aspects.

About SiteGround and HostGator

With an industry experience of 12 years, SiteGround is now powering a huge number of websites. For all these, it has been offering a wider set of hosting-related services. Quite noted for the transparency and the extra performance its servers give, SiteGround attracted professionals’ attention in somewhat no time. Despite the higher performance as well as resources, prices of SiteGround have been an attractive factor too. Headquartered in Bulgaria, the company is now having more than 320 employees.

HostGator, at some time, was used synonymously with web hosting.  Founded by Brent Oxley in 2003, HostGator has a clear track record for providing traditional hosting services, with state-of-the-art infrastructure and support. It is now owned by Endurance International Group, which is a web hosting conglomerate that owns many popular web hosts. As of now, HostGator has a huge client-base, which is still growing. It has been noted for ultra-affordable pricing plans.

SiteGround vs HostGator — What They Offer?

In the widened product catalogue of SiteGround, we can find both the common and new forms of web hosting. It is, for instance, offering Shared Web Hosting, Reseller Hosting and Dedicated Server Hosting — which are obviously the traditional forms of web hosting. In addition to these, SiteGround gives you Cloud Hosting and dedicated hosting for WordPress and Joomla. It means, you get optimized infrastructure and support for hosting sites that are based upon WordPress or Joomla. In short, the product range of SiteGround is quite comprehensive and suitable for the needs of new-gen web masters.

As we come to the available services from HostGator, there’s the same satisfaction. HostGator offers shared hosting, reseller hosting, VPS hosting, dedicated server hosting, cloud hosting and WordPress specific hosting. In addition to these, you also get domain names from HostGator, making the company an all-in-one shop for professionals. Indeed, the host has different pricing plans have for each hosting, but they are quite affordable when compared with status quo of web hosting industry. In other words, HostGator serves all the required hosting recipes at a reasonable rate.

Pricing Structure of HostGator and SiteGround

To understand the affordability of pricing, we can take the case of shared hosting from SiteGround Hosting Services. With three different plans for different needs, SiteGround is suitable for professionals. The first plan is StartUp and it provides single-domain hosting with 10 GB of storage space; it costs you $3.95 per month. If you go with the second plan named GrowBig that costs $7.95 per month, you are going to get 20 GB of space and multiple domain hosting. In the third plan called GoGeek that is priced at $14.95/month, you get 30 GB of space and multiple-domain hosting, along with geeky features. All these plans have some cool essential features to offer, such as free domain name.

Hatchling, Baby and Business are the three shared hosting plans you’d get from HostGator Hosting services. In first, it lets you host a single website for $5.56 per month. If you go with the Baby plan that has a monthly rent of $7.96, you can host unlimited websites, accompanied with unlimited bandwidth. The third plan Business has some premium set of options too. It’s priced at $11.96 per month and gives you an SSL, dedicated IP address and toll-free number. It is to be noted that all the rates are applicable only when you purchase the services for three years.

Availability of Features

Here’s a quick choice between SiteGround vs HostGator! Apart from the free site builder and quick installation script, HostGator does not offer many features. However, in SiteGround, there are sets of features included in each plan. In the basic plan, you get essential features such as free domain, free SSD, free SSL and free CDN etc. All these features are quite useful for an upcoming professional. So, you can simply choose SiteGround for features — there’s no doubt on that.

Up time and Performance

If you were to make a selection between SiteGround vs HostGator based on up time, it’s not possible. Both the web hosts are offering the same level of up time, all the time — 100%. However, from the perspective of performance, we may have another perspective.

When compared to HostGator servers, SiteGround has a well-performing set of web servers to offer. It has many reasons for that, too. First of all, SiteGround Hosting Services has its data centers set up in different continents and the appropriate one is chosen depending on the user’s location.

Next up, we should take into account SiteGround’s hardware in use. To support the up-to-date server-side software, SiteGround makes use of SSDs in all the packages, which is a matter of advantage. In short, if you have hosted your site/blog in SiteGround, you’ll have better performance and speed.

Reliability and Support

There’s no big deal about reliability in the SiteGround vs HostGator duo. Both the firms have a money-back guarantee, in case if you are not satisfied with the provided services. Also, the user sentiments about both the web hosts are a lot more promising.

In case of customer support, SiteGround and HostGator are equal in the first look. You get different ways to reach the customer support team. In both, you get live chat and telephone-based support, however, in SiteGround, you also get a clean ticketing option too. Despite the minor changes, you can choose any of these.

The Final Word on SiteGround vs HostGator

In light of above-mentioned factors, SiteGround seems to be the better option. The hosting packages are affordable than the pricing structure of HostGator. Not only that, each hosting plan comprises awesome features such as free backup, free domain, SSD servers etc. These all are helpful from the site performance point of view, we bet. So, with all these points in mind, you can go with SiteGround Hosting Services.
